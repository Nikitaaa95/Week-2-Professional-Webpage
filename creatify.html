Building My First AI Video Ad with Creatify

Nikita Thomas

AI Tools Video Marketing Haas Creatify

When Creatify came to Haas, I was the first person in the room with my hand up. I spend a lot of time thinking about where AI fits inside operations and product workflows — but video marketing had always felt like a different world. Long production timelines, expensive agencies, a lot of guesswork about what would actually resonate. Creatify was promising to collapse all of that.

The challenge was simple: use their platform to build a real video ad. The brand? Entirely up to me.

Meet Mighty Paw

I built an ad for a fictional dog food company called Mighty Paw — starring my very real dog. The brand brief I had in my head: premium, playful, the kind of thing you'd see in a Whole Foods aisle. I wanted to see if Creatify could actually capture a brand voice without me knowing anything about video production.

Spoiler: it did. Within a few minutes, I had a polished, ready-to-run video ad. The kind that would have taken a small team days to produce.

"We're the only AI-powered platform that helps marketers and creative teams create, test, and improve video ads — without long production timelines or guesswork. With Creatify, you can turn any product link into dozens of ready-to-run video ads in seconds."

Creatify

What I Actually Noticed

What impressed me wasn't just the speed — it was the reduction in the decision paralysis that usually kills creative projects early. When you can generate ten variations in the time it normally takes to brief a designer, the conversation shifts. You stop debating concepts and start reacting to real outputs. That's a fundamentally different creative process.

From an operations lens, the implications are significant. Marketing teams that currently rely on agency cycles or internal production queues could move dramatically faster. For a growth-stage company — or a healthcare startup trying to run localized campaigns across markets — that kind of velocity matters.

Why It Stuck With Me

I came into the challenge curious. I left thinking about the organizational change that comes with tools like this — not just the time saved, but the workflows that need to be rebuilt around them. Who owns iteration? How do you test at scale without losing brand consistency? Those are the questions I find most interesting, and Creatify made them feel very real, very fast.
