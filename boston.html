Places & Recs

Boston

Two years, all four seasons, more cannolis than I should admit.

By Season

Summer

Rent kayaks on the Charles

There is nothing better than being out on the water with the skyline right there in front of you. Rent from Community Rowing or the Esplanade — go on a weekday if you can, the weekend crowds are real.

Baseball Season

You must go to Fenway

  • Drink outside first — the bars around Fenway are part of the experience
  • Show up in the 3rd or 4th inning, lines are gone and the energy is already there
  • Sing Sweet Caroline. Non-negotiable.
  • Buy a hot dog
  • Cheer whenever everyone else does — you'll figure it out
Winter

Seaport winter fairs

The Seaport district goes all out in winter. The fairs are cozy, the lights are good, and it's one of those nights where the cold actually makes it feel more festive. Bundle up and go.

Where to Eat & Drink

Panza

Order: Lobster Ravioli

Don't skip this one. The lobster ravioli is the reason you go.

Lucca — North End

Order: Any pasta

Everything in the North End is good pasta, but Lucca is my personal pick. Walk the neighborhood after — it feels like a different city.

Mike's vs. Bova — Cannoli

Order: A cannoli (obviously)

I've done the research. Mike's is the classic, but Bova wins on manageability — you can actually finish a whole one. Everyone I've taken has loved Bova more. Take that how you will.

Neptune Oyster

Order: Oysters

Best oysters in the city. Go when the weather's bad — the line gets long otherwise. Fair warning: everything else on the menu is fine but a little overpriced.

Row 34

Order: Lobster Roll

The lobster roll. Full stop.

Soju Bar

Go on a weekend

Great vibe on a weekend night. Caveat: it was the only real Asian scene I found while I was there, so take that with a grain of salt — but I always had a good time.

Parla

Cocktails

Go for drinks. Lovely space, good cocktail list.

Drink

Cocktails

No menu — they ask what you're in the mood for and make something. The whole experience is the point.
