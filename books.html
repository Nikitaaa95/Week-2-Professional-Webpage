Reading List

Top 10 Books

The ones that stuck. In no particular order — except that Pride and Prejudice is always first.

01

Pride and Prejudice

Jane Austen

Witty, slow-burn romance with one of literature's sharpest heroines. Elizabeth Bennet's mind is the real love interest.

02

To Kill a Mockingbird

Harper Lee

Scout's voice carries enormous moral weight without ever feeling preachy, and Atticus Finch is one of the all-time great fathers in fiction.

03

Howl's Moving Castle

Diana Wynne Jones

Cozy magic, a dramatic wizard, and a heroine cursed into being old. Pure comfort reading.

04

Little Women

Louisa May Alcott

The March sisters feel like real people you grew up with. Jo's ambition and Beth's quiet warmth stick with you forever.

05

The Chosen

Chaim Potok

A quiet, beautiful story about friendship, faith, and fathers. The kind of book that sneaks up on you emotionally.

06

Of Mice and Men

John Steinbeck

Short, devastating, and impossible to forget. George and Lennie's dream of the rabbits will gut you every time.

07

Dune

Frank Herbert

Epic worldbuilding that actually earns its complexity. Politics, ecology, and prophecy on a desert planet.

08

Ella Enchanted

Gail Carson Levine

A fairy-tale retelling that's clever, funny, and gives Cinderella actual agency. (And way better than the movie.)

09

Yellowface

R.F. Kuang

A sharp, uncomfortable satire of publishing and identity. You'll keep reading even as you wince.

10

The Taming of the Shrew

William Shakespeare

Sparring dialogue at its finest. Complicated by modern standards, but the wordplay between Kate and Petruchio is electric.
